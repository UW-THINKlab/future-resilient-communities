Project Overview

The Future Resilient Communities Research Project is a multi-disciplinary research project funded by the National Science Foundation (NSF) Smart and Connected Communities (S&CC) program. The project aims to develop a novel framework for enhancing the resilience of communities to natural and human-made disasters through the integration of advanced communication technologies, data analytics, and community engagement.

Research Team

The project brings together a diverse team of researchers from the fields of civil and industrial engineering, computer science, and social sciences to address the complex challenges of building resilient communities in the face of increasing disaster risks. The research team is collaborating with local communities to co-develop and implement innovative solutions that leverage the latest advances in technology and data science to enhance community resilience.

This project foundationally integrates social, technical and community partners by engaging all of them in all phases of the work.The research project proposes 4 thrusts ( Thrust 1, Thrust 2, Thrust 3, and Thrust 4.) to answer various research questions, and each thrust will be led by one or two PI/Co-PI(s) on the team and will engage all team members.

Thrust 1 - Robust communications solutions

Develop robust off-grid community-based communication networks that can function during internet failure.

Answer questions: How can we design community communications infrastructure to be robust to disasters? How can we best build computing services that leverage community-held infrastructure during a disaster?

Thrust 2 - Building community social networks

Conduct random sample surveys to collect information about people's sharing behavior and their social ties within a community, and develop novel models that infer social ties for the entire community, to generates an initial social network snapshot for a community.

Answer questions: Can we obtain information on community-based social networks without actually collecting detailed social tie data? If yes, how to do it?

Thrust 3 - Design for information-resource sharing

Understand how social network structures affect information-resource sharing, as well as formulate strategies that will lead to efficient information-resource sharing.

Answer questions: How to disseminate information efficiently during a disaster?

Thrust 4 - Dynamic "Map your neighborhood"

Integrate the results of Thrusts 1-3 and use them to co-design and pilot-test applications with two communities.

Answer questions: How does the proposed dynamic map-your-neighborhood program better support robust communication and efficient information-resource sharing during a disaster?
