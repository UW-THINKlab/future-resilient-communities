Project Overview

The Future Resilient Communities Research Project is a multi-disciplinary research project funded by the National Science Foundation (NSF) Smart and Connected Communities (S&CC) program. The project aims to develop a novel framework for enhancing the resilience of communities to natural and human-made disasters through the integration of advanced communication technologies, data analytics, and community engagement.

Thrust 1 - Robust communications solutions

Develop robust off-grid community-based communication networks that can function during internet failure.

Answer questions: How can we design community communications infrastructure to be robust to disasters? How can we best build computing services that leverage community-held infrastructure during a disaster?

Thrust 2 - Building community social networks

Conduct random sample surveys to collect information about people's sharing behavior and their social ties within a community, and develop novel models that infer social ties for the entire community to generate an initial social network snapshot.

Answer questions: Can we obtain information on community-based social networks without actually collecting detailed social tie data? If yes, how?

Thrust 3 - Design for information-resource sharing

Understand how social network structures affect information-resource sharing, and formulate strategies that lead to efficient information-resource sharing.

Answer questions: How to disseminate information efficiently during a disaster?

Thrust 4 - Dynamic "Map your neighborhood"

Integrate the results of Thrusts 1 - 3 and use them to co-design and pilot-test applications with two communities.

Answer questions: How does the proposed dynamic map-your-neighborhood program better support robust communication and efficient information-resource sharing during a disaster?
