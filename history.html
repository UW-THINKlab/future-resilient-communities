Project History

Tracing the evolution of our research on community resilience.

2016

Initial Interest

In 2016, Cynthia Chen and Dan Abramson began to develop a collaborative interest in researching social-technical systems resilience at the community scale as it might become expressed in a major disruption to communications and transportation. Cynthia had been working on disaster resilience by both developing novel resilience indices with big data and modeling the failure propagation process on interdependent infrastructure systems. Dan had been working both internationally and locally for ten years on earthquake and tsunami recovery and hazards mitigation planning. The two felt that a community-based focus was important. They undertook to co-advise an incoming PhD student in Urban Design and Planning, Katherine Idziorek, to work on this topic with them. With degrees in both architectural and urban design and planning, and 8 years of professional practice in community engagement for urban design and transportation projects, Katie was well-qualified to contribute to this work.

2017 (April - June)

Community Engagement

In April 2017, John Temple and Roger Roffman (both of Laurelhurst residents and Roger is a UW emeritus professor) approached Cynthia, with a proposal of having a student from UW to conduct a community-based earthquake preparedness project in Laurelhurst. Cynthia approached Dan and Katie about the idea, and they met with John to discuss how their research and professional experience might serve the Laurelhurst effort.
In June 2017, UW team (Katie and Cynthia) met with Laurelhurst LEAP leadership including John Temple and Roger Roffman. Many meetings and conversations with the community since then ...

2017 (Continue)

TOMNET Project and Funding

2017 is also the first year for TOMNET funding and Cynthia put in a proposal for "Attitudes and Trust in Leveraging Integrated Sociotechnical Systems for Enhancing Community Adaptive Capacity Phase I". This is the first funded project for Katie's dissertation. With TOMNET funding, 2017 is the first year that our focus on community-based peer-to-peer resource and information sharing started.

2017-2018

Resilience Exploration

During the 2017-2018 academic year, Dan, Cynthia and Katie also developed a proposal for Bullitt Foundation funding to work with both Laurelhurst and Westport, a small rural community on the Washington coast that had taken remarkable steps to prepare for a catastrophic tsunami, by rebuilding its elementary school as a tsunami vertical evacuation structure. The successful proposal supported the mounting of an applied practical planning studio course and community workshops to explore the need for and potential of new communications infrastructure to enhance resilience in the face of these hazards.

2020

NSF-JST Planning Project (started)

NSF-JST planning project came in led by Cynthia Chen and Dan served as Co-PI with two new other Co-PIs, Payman Arahshahi and Sreeram Kannan of ECE UW.

2021

Global Innovation Fund Support

TOMNET funding continued for Phase II, III, and IV in 2021, when Katie finished her dissertation and supported her to conduct household surveys on attitudes towards local resource and information sharing given social relationships and disruptions to transportation.
Following Katie's graduation, Cynthia and Dan received Global Innovation Fund support to explore international comparative study of this topic with partners in Japan. The GIF study built capacity for US-Japan collaboration on community-based resilience work.

2022

NSF-JST Planning Project (continued)

The NSF-JST planning project continued analysis of the community survey results, in combination with a comparative survey in Nagoya, Japan, and exploring further the requirements of physical communications infrastructure and software design for P2P resource and information sharing.

2022-2023

NSF LEAP-HI Project (continued)

NSF LEAP-HI funding on creating adaptable societies led by Cynthia Chen and Dan Abramson serves as a co-PI. The project focused on how to create adaptable systems that will work together. NSF-JST and NSF LEAP-HI funding and TOMNET funding also supported a new Urban Design and Planning student, Cristina Cano-Calhoun, for her MUP thesis to conduct qualitative interviews of partner community stakeholders for P2P sharing.

2023-2027

NSF S&CC Project

Current NSF S&CC project led by Cynthia Chen, with Dan Abramson and new Co-PIs: Kurtis Heimerl, Shuai Huang and Tyler McCormick.
